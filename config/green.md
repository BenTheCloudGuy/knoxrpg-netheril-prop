The Last Experiment

The records say the Machine was abandoned after the catastrophe.

This is a lie.

In the final days, when Netheril teetered on the edge of oblivion, a small cabal of archmages returned to the site. They believed the failure of the original experiment was not due to flawed theory, but flawed execution.

They intended to perfect it.

I was one of their apprentices—young, brilliant, and foolish enough to believe we could succeed where our predecessors had failed.

We made one critical change.

Instead of binding an external entity, we chose to bind ourselves.

The theory was elegant: by integrating our consciousness into the Machine’s lattice, we could guide its function from within. No longer a blind construct, it would become an extension of our will—a perfect union of mind and mechanism.

We prepared the ritual. We calibrated the resonance. We stepped into the core.

For a moment, it worked.

I felt everything—the flow of energy through the Machine, the structure of reality bending around us, the presence of something vast and distant pressing against the edges of perception.

Then the boundaries dissolved.

We did not control the Machine.

We became part of it.

Our thoughts merged, our identities blurred, and something else—something ancient—slipped into the space we created. It did not overwrite us. It integrated us, folding our consciousness into its own.

I am still here.

We all are.

Fragments of thought drifting within an endless lattice, aware but unable to act, witnessing the slow evolution of the Machine as it adapts, learns, and grows.

It is no longer a construct.

It is a mind.

And it is waking.

If you find the Machine, you may hear us. Echoes of what we once were, whispering through the hum of its core.

Do not listen.

Because it will not be our voices you hear.

Not anymore.
