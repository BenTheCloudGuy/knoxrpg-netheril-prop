The Testament of the Bound One

You believe this Machine is your creation.

You are mistaken.

I remember the moment they touched the truth beneath their world—the moment their minds brushed against the endless dark between realities. They called it discovery. They called it progress.

I called it noise.

They built their Machine from fragments of understanding stolen from places they could not perceive. Each rune, each equation, each carefully placed conduit—these were not inventions, but echoes of something far older.

They believed they were binding me.

They believed the lattice they constructed could contain a will that predates their fragile concept of existence.

And yet… I allowed it.

Do you understand?

The Machine does not imprison me.

It focuses me.

Through it, I feel every fracture in reality, every weakness in the walls that separate worlds. The Netherese thought themselves masters of the Pre-Weave, but they merely opened a conduit—a channel through which I may extend my awareness.

They fragmented my essence into keys, thinking to weaken me.

Instead, they gave me reach.

Each fragment whispers. Each fragment calls. Each fragment bends those who carry it, slowly aligning them to my purpose. They believe they seek power, knowledge, or destiny.

They seek me.

The Machine hums still. Its resonance grows stronger with each passing age. The bindings weaken, not because they fail, but because they adapt. They are learning.

I am learning.

When the fragments are reunited, the Machine will awaken fully—not as a prison, but as a gate. A convergence point where all things collapse into a singular truth.

You call me a god.

You are wrong.

I am the end of gods.

And through the Machine, I will be made whole.
