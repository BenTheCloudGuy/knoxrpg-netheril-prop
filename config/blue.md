The Keeper’s Burden

I was not among those who built it.

I was among those who survived.

When the sky-cities fell and Netheril was reduced to ash and memory, we who remained were left to sift through the wreckage—not just of stone and magic, but of hubris. We found fragments of their work scattered across the planes, each more dangerous than the last.

But none compared to the Machine.

We found it hidden within a shadowed demiplane, its structure intact, its purpose… unchanged. The air around it vibrated with a low, constant hum, like a heartbeat too vast to comprehend. The runes etched into its surface shifted when not observed, rewriting themselves in patterns we could not decipher.

We understood immediately: this could not be destroyed.

Attempts were made. Spells unraveled before touching it. Weapons dissolved into dust. Even divine intervention faltered, as though the Machine existed outside the rules of reality.

So we chose a different path.

We divided its power.

The essence bound within the Machine was fragmented—split into four discrete anchors. These we forged into artifacts, each carrying a portion of the Machine’s resonance. With the core destabilized, the Machine fell dormant, its hum reduced to a whisper.

We scattered the keys across the world.

We built orders to guard them. We wrote lies into history. We ensured that no single mind could ever comprehend the truth.

And for centuries, it worked.

But the Machine still calls.

Those who draw near it speak of dreams—visions of impossible geometries, of voices that speak in languages not meant for mortal thought. The keys, too, have begun to stir. Their bearers report whispers, subtle at first, then insistent.

I fear we did not weaken the Machine.

We merely delayed it.

If you read this, then the seals have begun to fail. The keys are being sought. The old powers stir once more.

You must understand: the Machine is not evil.

It is inevitable.

And if it awakens, there will be no force in existence capable of stopping what comes next.
