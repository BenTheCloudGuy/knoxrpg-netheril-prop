The Architect’s Confession

I write this not as a warning, but as a record—so that when we are gone, the truth remains unmarred by myth.

We did not stumble upon the Machine. We conceived it.

In the final centuries of Netheril’s ascent, when mythallars bent gravity and time alike, we grew dissatisfied with the Weave. It was a lattice—beautiful, yes—but imposed. A system governed by unseen hands, restrictive in its logic. We believed magic should not be borrowed, but commanded.

It was Archmage Valryx who first spoke of the “substrate beneath reality”—aether unbound, predating the gods. We called it the Pre-Weave, a raw field of existence that could be shaped without divine permission. Through it, we believed we could surpass even the gods.

The Infernal Machine was our proof.

At its core, the device is not a mechanism of gears or levers, but of resonance. It harmonizes with the essence of a being—divine, mortal, or otherwise—and draws it into a state of perfect containment. A prison not of walls, but of alignment. Nothing escapes because nothing can exist outside its imposed truth.

We tested it first on lesser entities. Elementals unraveled into silence. Outsiders collapsed into inert echoes. Encouraged, we turned our attention to something greater.

That was our sin.

We believed we could bind a god—not kill it, not banish it—but own it. We prepared the ritual, constructed the Machine within a demiplane shielded from planar interference, and initiated the sequence.

For a moment—just a moment—we succeeded.

The entity was drawn in, its essence fracturing, its power stabilizing within the lattice.

Then it noticed us.

Not as ants notice a storm, but as a storm notices the hand that shaped it.

The backlash was… immeasurable. Minds shattered. Flesh dissolved into patterns of light and shadow. Entire sections of the Machine twisted into forms that should not exist.

We contained it—but not as we intended.

The Machine still functions. It still binds. But it is no longer ours. It has become something… aware. Not alive, but not inert. It listens.

If you find it, do not attempt to use it.

We were the greatest minds of Netheril.

And we failed.
